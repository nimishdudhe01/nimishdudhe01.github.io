Why Am I Learning Rust, What Makes Me Write This?

As I mentioned in my earlier blog, I’m exploring different things to find something I really love and In the moment I’m actually trying to learn about blockchain. After having a discussion with a friend of mine who has been in blockchain development space for quite a while, he suggested me to go with the Rust Language and her we are, I started learning the Rust language. At the time of writing this blog I already worked on quite a few beginner level Rust programs like the default first program “Hello, World!”, variables, data types, type inference and type annotations, if else and branching, user input, etc. And I don’t think we as programmers will ever get bored of this “Hello, World!” program. Moving on to the Learning Part.

What are the basic things you need to learn a Programming Language? or anything new.

  1. The Will to Do So (The Spark of Intent): Seeing you come so far, I can clearly see the Spark of Intent in you about learning something new.
  2. Curiosity (The Inquisitive Catalyst): As programmers we usually are curious about what we can do with do with a particular technology. How it can be used, what problems can be solved using this, what things can be made more efficient, etc.
  3. Resources Galore (The Learning Arsenal): Accumulating the different resources (lectures, books, code samples, documentation, etc.) for working our way up learning something.
  4. A Clear Plan of Action (The Guiding Blueprint): One of the most important things. Think of it as, we can’t go making full-fledged market ready products in our first time of writing code in a new language, right? We first need to learn different concepts of the programming language, a bit of experience, etc.
  5. Practice (The Refinement Forge): Last but not the least, something that differentiates the expert from the novice. Knowing everything does keep you in a place above a lot of people, But it can’t take the place of someone who is experienced. Just like Bruce Lee said
I am not afraid of a person who knows 10000 kicks. But I am afraid of a person who knows one kick but practices it for 10000 times.

For the points 3 and 4, We will be leveraging the power of ChatGPT.

Making a Plan of Action — Method I

  1. Fire up ChatGPT, and Open a new chat so that this particular chat is used only to learn the language.
  2. Enter the prompt “Make me a 4 week plan for learning beginner level <your desired programming language>”
  3. Submit the prompt and you’ll have your answer. Which might look something like this.

Making a Plan of Action — Method II

  1. Fire up Google. Search beginner level books of the language or subject you’re tying to learn.
  2. Select a book whose table of content is clear, detailed and technical(You won’t need to spend a penny).
  3. Now that you have the detailed ToC, We will move on to the next part.

Getting the Resources!

Fire up ChatGPT again. And Make it teach you the concepts and programming.
